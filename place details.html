  1. Feryal garden

    2. Faryal Historical Park is located in the East District of Port Said, on an area of ​​21,904 meters in 1869, during the reign of Khedive Ismail. It is a witness to the history of Port Said. Al-Watan provides information about the historical park.

    1. Its opening witnessed a solemn ceremony during the general opening of the Suez Canal after it was dug in the presence of princes and kings of the world. It was named after Princess Ferial, the eldest daughter of King Farouk I.

    2. The park overlooks the course of the Suez Canal from the northern side, and it consists of three platforms with green spaces established at the time of the opening.

    3. The ceremony was attended, according to historical references, Khedive Ismail, Fernand De Lesseps, and Eugenie, Empress of France, who named a street after her. Prince Henry, brother of the King of the Netherlands, the ambassadors of England and Russia in Astana, Prince Muhammad Tawfiq, the Crown Prince, Prince Tousun, son of Muhammad Said Pasha, Sherif Pasha, Nubar Pasha, and Prince Abdelkader Al-Jazaery, on the grand platform.

    4. Major General Aad Al-Ghadban, Governor of Port Said, decided to develop it after it remained for many years without development and at that time angered those interested in the history of the park for fear of obliterating its features and removing rare historical trees and plants in it, ranging in age from 70 to 100 years, such as eucalyptus trees, poplars, and taxidium, Albusina, and others, and these trees are stationed in the northwest square of the garden, and around its fence, which makes it difficult to move.

    5. The governor’s decision to develop the park was implemented, and the development work included green spaces, flowers, plants and trees in aesthetic shapes, and a red mill that would serve as a center for surveillance cameras. A wooden bridge and a number of murals that tell historical excerpts from the garden and the history of Port Said, in addition to a theater, an Andalusian-style mosque, special seating areas for the elderly, and decorative columns. The area surrounding the garden has also been raised and the buildings facade painted. The garden also has an integrated camera monitoring system and specialized companies. In hygiene and security.

    6. It was inaugurated during the visit of Dr. Mostafa Madbouly, the Prime Minister, last November to Port Said.

    7. The garden also witnessed taking pictures of weddings, engagement parties and events, due to the distinguished tourist and service edifice that it witnesses, in light of the Corona pandemic, and the closure of wedding halls for several months.

  2. salt mountains

    3. 120 minutes from the heart of Cairo towards the Cairo-Ismailia desert road, after you pass about 142 kilometers, you will find yourself reaching the city of resistance Port Said, you will then take the ferry to enter Port Fouad and there anyone from inside the city will tell you your destination, which is Port Fouad Salinas or the Salt Mountains. At first glance since you arrived at the place, you will think that you are in the Alps in Italy or perhaps in Switzerland, but what you feel is just an optical illusion because you are still in the city of Port Fouad in Egypt, which is located a few kilometers from the surface of the Mediterranean Sea. The salt pans in Port Fouad, Port Said Governorate, have a story that began since its establishment in 1859 by the French campaign in a primitive way, on a large area estimated at 6 million and 822 thousand square meters, but then it was intervened to develop it in 1956, and with the nationalization of the Suez Canal it was given to a company Max Salines with a contract to exploit it for a period of 20 years ending in 1975, in which the company works to collect salt in it, and the volume of raw salt production in the navigation reached about 300 thousand tons annually, which is manufactured, and the export of nearly 100 thousand tons of washed salt, and the production of 50 thousand tons Raw salt and supplying about 150 thousand tons to the local Egyptian market.
